Task 1: Identifying Your Audience

In consideration of my topic, I think the topic of my website could potentially be helpful to many people, as most people have an online presence. However, I have decided to appeal to a younger audience of pre-teens and teens.

My reasoning for this is because these people will be newer to joining communities and creating online social identities. So too, informing them on what a social identity is and how to safely go about it will be extremely beneficial to them specifically (I believe).

AUDIENCE RATIONALE
AGE Writing for teens allows the opportunity to be a bit more mature in the way that I write. However, I must consider that they would not want dense language. To hold a teens attention is hard, thus, short, interactive methods of informing them would probably be preferred.
GENDER I am not appealing to any one gender in general. If anything, I would use as inclusive language as possible as to not disregard one's identity.
LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY I will be writing in English, however, I can acknowledge that this really limits the audiences my website will reach.
EDUCATION LEVEL My audience will be younger, but not children. Therefore I can use informative, elaborate language to an extent. However, I must keep language basic, and display information in a way that is clear, readable and easily understood.
ATTITUDE TOWARDS WRITER I will write in a fashion to not scare, but warn and create a wariness to my audience. I do not want the website to cause the reader major concern, but to create a reaction that brings awareness to the topic.
KNOWLEDGE OF TOPIC I do not expect my website to be geared to experts of the topic. Rather, I imagine that my audience will have basic knowledge, this can be used as an educational tool to reinforce and expand upon what I imagine they will have been told by other seniors in their life previously.
AUDIENCE REACTION As aforementioned, I wish to an extent for my audience to be wary of their actions online and the way they present themselves. I hope that it would encourage readers/users to change their own presence to a more secure, as well as question and consider their digital footprint.

Task 3: EXPLORING THE AUDIENCE'S NEEDS IN RELATION TO DESIGN

User Stories

I began by using the UK Government website to learn how to write user stories for my previously established personas. To build a user story you must include the actor, the narrative and the goal. The actor in this case being theoretical users of the website.

Here are some examples of my user stories, as adhering to the guidelines on the website:



"As a school teacher, I want to access tools to aid and develop what I am teaching in my class, so that my pupils can have a broad knowledge on a concept I am not extensive on."



"As a pre-teen without social media, I want to know how to safely use social media as a way of communicating, so that I can talk to my friends online in a safe and secure manner."



"As an avid social media user, I want to know what to do if something were to go wrong in my online communities, so that I can continue using online communities as a safe-space comfortably."



User Need Statements

I found that a user need statements follow a similar path in this stage of design thinking. In this, you identify who may be a potential user, their need from this website is and their final goal they wish to achieve in accessing said website.



The Laddering Technique

The laddering technique focuses on an A-C-V sequence. This "indicates the relationship between a product attribute and a core value" (Gibbons, 2019).

ATTRIBUTE:

Likely to be the first thing a user notices in the website, but they should not be the only focus in creating a product.

CONSEQUENCES:

Understanding the impact of certain attributes rather than solely their aesthetic.

VALUE:

The reasons people would use, recommend and return to a product.

I did not conduct an actual laddering interview, however, I grasped the importance of asking why? In response to most outcomes. Considering every aspect of the website and why it is important?

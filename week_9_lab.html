VIDEO 1:

This is the version of my video that I would argue is the most optimised for web use.

Previous to this form of the video, there were individual clips which I trimmed, split, removed sound from, and placed in a narrative order.

VIDEO 2:

For this version of my video I cut majority of the content out and decided to add sound and motion effects.

I slowed down the clip of the girl's eyes, and added a siren sound effect when the fake news article appeared on screen.

The manipulation of motion and addition of sound can have emotive effects, and progress a narrative. However, they can also have negative effects, such as in this case.

The addition of the siren is overbearing, and distracts the viewer from the video. It also causes issues with accessibility, analysed more in week 10's tasks.

Additionally I exported this to 480p resolution. This is an extremely low quality, meaning the video can have a maximum of 60 frames per second.

This results in an extremely low quality video, not suitable for users used to high, 4k quality that is easily accessed today.



VIDEO 3:

This version of the video is not any different to the first video, editing-wise.

However, I have, again, lowered the quality of the video.

Low quality videos often suffer from pixelation, reduced clarity and distorted colours. This can make it challenging to discern details of the video used.

It also effects the audio, so it impacts the ability to understand the narration and understanding of narrative.

Overall, the first use is the optimal as it allows for the best comprehension of this educational/ informative content, and will attain the most enjoyment regarding the user experience.
